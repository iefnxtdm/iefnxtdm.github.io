About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL), specializing in advanced building thermal technologies. I complished PhD study in Worcester Polytechnic Institute. My research focuses on improving Building energy efficiency through innovative materials, systems, and data-driven modeling. I also conducted research on the structural analysis of offshore wind turbine foundations during my M.S. studies at Hunan University. I am actively seeking academic collaborations on research proposals and publications.

My research interests include:

  • Thermal Energy Storage: Phase Change Materials (PCMs) and Sorbent materials
  • Daytime Radiative Cooling
  • Thermal Switches
  • Novel building insulation materials
  • Machine Learning Applications in Building Energy Systems
  • Structural Analysis of Offshore Wind Turbine Foundations

Highlight #1: Thermal Switches based Dynamic Building Envelopes

We developed a passive thermal switch embedded in building insulation that automatically changes its heat-flow path based on ambient temperature. This technology enhances the effectiveness of PCMs in thermal energy storage, helping to reduce building heating and cooling loads.

  • Passive, insertable thermal switches are developed and experimentally characterized.
  • One switch per 100 in^2 of insulation provides a thermal conductivity switching ratio of 5.7.
  • Switches enhance utilization of PCM-thermal storage and improve indoor thermal comfort.

Highlight #2: thermo-optically responsive SS-PCM coatings

Our thermo-optical solid–solid phase change material (SS-PCM) coatings absorb excess heat when the building is warm and release it when cooler, moderating indoor temperature swings and lowering HVAC energy demand. We applied a bioinspired surface-optimization approach and machine-learning-based design to develop porous SS-PCM coatings with enhanced optical and thermal performance for adaptive building envelopes.

  • Thermo-optical solid–solid PCM exhibits switchable cooling and solar heating effects in response to the ambient temperature.
  • The SS-PCM can achieve radiative cooling effect when it integrates Polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) and Silver(Ag).
